Capital Intake & Procurement-to-Pay Transformation

Business Analyst — Budget-to-Pay (B2P) Transformation Program

Situation

As-Is Budget-to-Pay processes across capital intake, procurement, vendor management, and invoicing/payments were fragmented and duplicative. Controls were applied downstream — catching errors after the fact — rather than upstream. Ownership and accountability across process steps were unclear, and audit defensibility was weak. Vendor record management in particular had no standalone defined process; it was handled piecemeal inside Accounts Payable and Procurement workflows.

My Role

Business Analyst leading end-to-end process analysis across capital intake, procurement, vendor setup, invoicing, and payment workflows. Day to day, this meant running process-mapping workshops, analyzing SAP and non-SAP transaction data, authoring SOPs and RACI matrices, building executive dashboards, and coordinating alignment across Finance, Procurement, Engineering, IT, and Legal.

Steps Taken

  1. Ran structured process-mapping workshops across six in-scope process areas: pre-planning/intake, capital intake & governance, prioritization & funding decision, procurement, vendor record management, and invoice & payments.
  2. Documented current-state (As-Is) workflows as swimlane diagrams, identifying 20+ control gaps, rework drivers, and approval bottlenecks.
  3. Analyzed SAP and intake data to trace root causes of delayed payments, duplicate reviews, and manual overrides.
  4. Authored 10+ SOPs and RACI matrices defining roles (Project Requestor, Division Director, Finance Capital Manager, City Treasurer, Executive Leadership Team, Legal, Risk) and escalation paths.
  5. Designed future-state (Ideal) workflows, including a newly defined standalone vendor-record-management process, reducing intake-to-approval cycle time.
  6. Built executive-ready decision briefs and dashboards used by Directors and Senior Managers to approve process and governance changes.
  7. Implemented structured action tracking and risk registers to reduce missed dependencies.
  8. Coordinated 8+ stakeholder groups (Finance, Procurement, Engineering, IT, Legal) through workshop sessions to align on scope, timelines, and approvals.

Obstacles & Resolution

Vendor record management had no process of its own, so bank, ownership, and name changes were handled inconsistently across AP and Procurement — resolved by designing a dedicated vendor-change workflow with classification logic (standard vs. enhanced verification). Controls were applied downstream, catching errors only after they occurred — resolved by re-sequencing the Ideal-state process so controls sit upstream, at intake and approval gates. SOPs were co-authored with another analyst on the transformation team.

Ideal

Fix: Dedicated vendor-record-management workflow with classification logic and standard/enhanced verification tiers, closing the accountability gap.

Problem Statement

Recommendation

Standardize a weekly/monthly reporting cadence with a structured reconciliation and data-quality checklist ahead of each close.

Options Considered
A. Continue ad hoc reportingRejected — no reconciliation gain
B. Standardized cadence + reconciliation controlsRecommended

Risk
